The Trap of the "Cheap" Plot: RERA Approved vs Non-RERA Land

Every weekend, hundreds of Puneites encounter newspaper ads offering "NA Plots" at unbelievably low prices in areas like Wagholi or Solapur Highway. These are usually 1 or 2-acre local developments. The catch? They almost never have a MahaRERA registration. Let's break down why a "cheap" non-RERA plot is the most expensive mistake you can make in 2026.

What is a Non-RERA Plot?

A non-RERA plot usually means the land has not undergone the rigorous legal scrutiny mandated by the government. The developer might have "Gunthewari" land or agricultural land that hasn't officially received Non-Agricultural (NA) conversion or PMRDA layout sanctioning.

When you buy this, you get a "Shared 7/12" or a notarized document. Legally, you do not have a registered, standalone title to that specific piece of earth.

The 3 Dangers of Local Non-RERA Layouts

  • No Bank Funding: Try getting an SBI or HDFC loan on a non-RERA plot. You will be rejected instantly. Banks only fund risk-free assets with individual 7/12 extracts.
  • The Amenity Illusion: Local builders promise clubhouses and roads. Without RERA, there is no legal deadline to build these. They collect the money and vanish, leaving you with a dirt patch.
  • Demolition Risk: PMRDA actively identifies and demolishes illegal, un-sanctioned layouts. Your life savings could be bulldozed in an afternoon.
