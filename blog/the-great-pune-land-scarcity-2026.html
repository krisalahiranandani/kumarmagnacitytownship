The Great Pune Land Scarcity (2026 Edition)

Published on March 11, 2026 | By Real Estate Research Desk

As Pune extends its urban boundaries further into the East and South corridors, a paradox is emerging: while there is plenty of land, there is a severe scarcity of "Investment-Ready" NA (Non-Agricultural) sanctioned plots.

The 2026 Supply Crunch

By mid-2026, with the completion of the Outer Ring Road Phase 1, accessibility to peripheral regions like Manjari and Handewadi has reached an all-time high. However, several factors have conspired to shrink the supply of premium gated communities:

  • Stringent RERA Compliance: Smaller, unorganized developers are struggling to meet the high standards of RERA, leading to a consolidation of the market toward branded developers.
  • PMRDA Master Plan: Zonal restrictions have prioritized residential clusters, leaving fewer pockets available for independent bungalow plotting.
  • Post-Metro Boom: Regions near the Metro Corridor have seen a 40% uptick in land acquisition by commercial conglomerates, leaving little room for individual residential assets.
