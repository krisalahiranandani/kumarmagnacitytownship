Understanding 7/12 Extract: Your Land Ownership Shield

In Maharashtra, the 7/12 extract (Saat Baara Utara) is the most important land ownership document. It's maintained by the Talathi (village revenue officer) and serves as conclusive proof of who owns a piece of land. When you buy an NA plot, this document must have YOUR name as the sole owner of your specific plot.

The Shared 7/12 Trap

Many small plot layouts in Pune offer "NA plots" but with a shared 7/12 extract — meaning 20-50 buyers are listed on ONE document. This creates serious problems:

  • No bank will finance a plot without individual 7/12
  • Disputes between co-owners can freeze your land
  • Resale becomes nearly impossible — new buyers avoid shared titles
  • No legal clarity on exact plot boundaries

FAQs — 7/12 Extract & Title

What is a 7/12 extract?

A government land record in Maharashtra that proves ownership. Contains survey number, owner name, area, and land-use status (NA/Agricultural).

Individual vs shared 7/12 — which is better?

Individual is the only acceptable option. Shared 7/12 means multiple people claim ownership of the same land parcel — banks won't finance, resale is hard, and disputes are common.

How can I verify a 7/12 online?

Visit bhulekh.mahabhumi.gov.in, select the district (Pune), taluka, and village, then enter the survey number. The 7/12 record will show current ownership details.
