The 2026 NRI Guide: How to Buy NA Land in Pune with Zero Risk

For Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), nothing compares to the emotional and financial security of owning a piece of the motherland. However, managing Indian real estate from 5,000 miles away comes with a unique set of anxieties—specifically regarding legalities and encroachment. Here is the 2026 guide to safely investing in NA Plots in Pune.

Rule 1: Only Buy NA (Non-Agricultural) Land

Under the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), NRIs and PIOs are legally barred from purchasing agricultural property. However, you have the absolute right to purchase residential NA (Non-Agricultural) bungalow plots. Ensure the developer provides the NA order document upfront.

Rule 2: The Encroachment Defense Strategy

The single biggest fear for any NRI land buyer is encroachment. If you buy a standalone plot in a remote location, it is essentially unguarded when you fly back. The modern and foolproof solution is the Branded Gated Township.

When you invest in Kumar Magnacity, you aren't just buying soil; you are buying into a 150-acre fortress. With 3-tier security, boom barriers, 24/7 CCTV surveillance, and a dedicated facility management team, your asset is fiercely protected 365 days a year.

Rule 3: Demand MahaRERA and PMC/PMRDA Approval

Remote buyers cannot afford ambiguity in titles. Only engage with developments that have a valid MahaRERA registration and full layout sanctioning from PMRDA. This guarantees that your funds are secure in an escrow account, rather than being diverted, and guarantees the delivery of amenities like clubhouses and roads.

Rule 4: The 100% Digital Buying Process

Top-tier developers like Kumar Properties have evolved. In 2026, you don't need to physically fly down to finalize your plot. From high-definition virtual site tours to digital documentation and online payment pathways via NRE/NRO accounts, the entire booking process is fully remote. You simply assign a trusted Power of Attorney (PoA) internally in India for the final stamp duty registration.
